      What this site can do

      Colour Mixer is a planning tool for real-world paint and ink mixes. Colours blend like pigment (Kubelka–Munk via spectral.js), not like screen RGB averages. Values are approximate mass-tones for planning — not brand-certified lab data.

      1. Choose a medium

      Switch at the top:

      • Watercolour — lighten with water; paper is your white
      • Gouache — water + white + body (matte / opaque)
      • Acrylic — white, black, covering power
      • Oil — same body controls; linen ground by default
      • Ink — dilution washes

      2. Build a mix

      • Tap paints from Limited, Brands, Common, Saved, or Custom
      • Adjust parts per paint; use water / white / black / body as the medium allows
      • Try sample mix loads Ultramarine + Burnt Sienna (classic grey)
      • Read the large swatch, hex, RGB, and recipe; copy hex anytime
      • Undo / Redo / Clear in the Mix panel header

      3. Paint tags

      Swatches show planning traits (inferred from pigment codes / names when not set by hand):

      • Opacity — transparent · semi · opaque
      • Staining — low · med · high
      • Granulating — sedimentary texture tendency

      4. Result tools

      • Mud score — clean / watch / risk from paint count, complements, chroma
      • Viewing light — CIE D65 (day), A (warm 2856 K), D75 (cool daylight). Spectral reflectance × illuminant SPD × CIE 1931 2° CMFs, Bradford-adapted to sRGB. Three-way strip compares all at once
      • Harmonies — complement, analogous, triad chips; tap to add the nearest limited-palette paint
      • Pigment vs RGB — Kubelka–Munk mix beside dumb RGB average
      • Save — history on this device only
      • Add glaze — push the mix onto the glaze stack
      • Export card — download a PNG recipe card
      • Share — copy a ?mix= link (and QR); open the link later to restore the recipe. Mobile may offer the system share sheet

      5. Glaze stack

      • Pick a ground (paper, canvas, linen, toned panel…)
      • Layers stack bottom → top with per-layer strength
      • Copy the composite hex or Use as mix base

      6. Reference

      • Hue wheel — mix and tube positions; click for a target hue and nearest paints
      • Harmony rings — optional arcs for analogous / complement / triad
      • Value strip — same mix lighter → fuller (water or body steps)
      • Temperature chip — warm / cool / neutral bias

      7. Paint library & dupe finder

      • Search every real paint across all brand lines by name or pigment code (e.g. PB29, quinacridone)
      • Filter by medium, single-pigment only (cleaner mixers), opacity, staining, or granulating
      • Dupes — pick any paint to see its closest matches in other brand lines of the same medium, ranked by ΔE (CIE76 in Lab)
      • Each dupe shows a property comparison — same pigment, opacity, staining, granulation — so you know why a substitute holds up (or won't)
      • No close match? A note suggests mixing the colour from your own palette instead
      • Mix sends any paint (or dupe) straight into the current mix

      8. Mixing decks

      • Themed recipe cards (skies, foliage, sunsets, autumn, water, skin tones, neutrals, florals)
      • Each card's swatch is mixed live by the pigment engine from its recipe — not a flat printed colour
      • The recipe shows each paint, its ratio (×parts), and the water level
      • Tap a card to load those paints, ratios and water into the mixer, then tweak (Undo restores your previous mix)

      9. Studio tools

      • Mixing chart — ratio grid from the first two paints in the mix
      • Compare A / B — park two recipes side by side and reload either
      • Print palette chart — limited tubes + sample 1:1 mixes
      • Share QR — same share link as a QR image
      • Match from photo — sample a colour, get suggested limited-palette recipes
      • Tips — medium-specific mixing advice
      • History — reload past saves from this browser

      10. Saved palettes

      Under Palette → Saved, store the paints currently in your mix as a named set. Tap a swatch to add one colour, or load the whole set.

      Keyboard & tips

      • ? or Shift+/ — open this help
      • Esc — close help
      • On phones, a sticky bar keeps hex + recipe visible while you scroll
      • Sister tool: Hue & Cry — Sanzo Wada combinations from a photo
